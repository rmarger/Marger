Paternity cases in Florida can be initiated by the mother, the alleged father, or the Florida Department of Revenue. Once filed, the process typically involves genetic testing, temporary orders for support and timesharing, and ultimately a final judgment establishing legal paternity.

**For fathers:** Establishing paternity gives you the legal right to seek timesharing and be involved in your child's life. Without it, you have no enforceable parental rights.

**For mothers:** A paternity judgment creates a legally enforceable child support obligation and gives your child the right to benefits including inheritance, health insurance, and Social Security.
