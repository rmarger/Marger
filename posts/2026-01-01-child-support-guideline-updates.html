The Algorithm Doesn't Know Best: AI in Family Law

April 2026

Hessert v. Hessert and Wieland-Pulayya, 6D26-0121 (March 20, 2026) — The Sixth DCA found that generative AI fabricated thirteen citations in submitted briefs. The opinion underscores generative AI's pitfalls, prompting the Sixth DCA to order the petitioner to show cause why sanctions should not follow.

Of the thirteen citations included in counsel's brief, four were accurate, four were irrelevant, and five were entirely fabricated. The court found this to be a serious breach of professional responsibility and ordered an explanation for why sanctions should not be imposed.

What this means for your case: Attorneys have an independent obligation to verify every authority they cite. Reliance on AI-generated legal research without verification is not a defense. Courts are increasingly intolerant of citation errors — whether caused by AI hallucinations or otherwise.
